How to make Aloo Gobhi Methi Ka Tuk At Home

Ingredients

  1. 4 (500 g) large potatoes with skin
  2. 200 g fenugreek
  3. Salt to taste
  4. 5 tbs (75 ml) oil
  5. 2 g mustard seeds
  6. 24 curry leaves
  7. 20 g ginger paste, strained
  8. 10 g garlic paste, strained
  9. 800 g cauliflower, cut into medium sized flowerettes
  10. 1 tsp (9 g) yellow chilli powder
  11. 1 tsp (9 g) amchur powder
  12. 1/2 tsp (2 g) cummin powder
  13. 1/2 tsp (1.5 g) fresh black pepper powder, coarsely ground
  14. A generous pinch of kasuri methi
  15. For the garnishing: 30 g fresh pomegranate seeds

Method

QUARTER the potatoes and then halve each quarter lengthwise. Blanch in salted boiling water until al dente (almost cooked, but not soft and squishy). Drain and keep aside. Sprinkle salt over the fenugreek and rub between the palms to reduce some of the bitterness. Wash in running water, drain and keep aside.

Heat oil in a kadai or wok and season with mustard seeds. When they crackle, add curry leaves. Stir for some time. Add the ginger paste and garlic paste. Stir-fry until the moisture evaporates. Then add fenugreek and stir for a few seconds. Add cauliflower, yellow chilli powder and salt. Stir well. Lower the heat. Cover and cook (for about six minutes) until al dente. Uncover and increase to medium heat. Add potatoes and stir-fry for five minutes. Sprinkle amchur powder, cummin powder, pepper powder and kasuri methi. Stir well. Check the seasoning and remove from heat.

To serve: Remove to a serving dish, garnish with pomegranate and serve with tandoori paratha, bidari paratha or puri.


Time required: 35 minutes

Serves: Four
